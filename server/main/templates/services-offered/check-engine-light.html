Check Engine Light Services

When to get a mechanic?

It's a good idea to have the engine light checked promptly after it comes on. This light often signals potential issues with the engine or emissions system, so getting it looked at early can help prevent any further problems down the road.

Common Causes:

Faulty Oxygen Sensor Indicates an issue with the oxygen sensor, which measures the amount of oxygen in the exhaust gases. This can affect fuel efficiency and emissions.

Mass Airflow Sensor (MAF) Problem Indicates issues with the MAF sensor, which measures the amount of air entering the engine. Problems with the MAF sensor can affect fuel mixture and engine performance.

Exhaust Gas Recirculation (EGR) Valve Malfunction recirculates a portion of the engine's exhaust gas back into the combustion chamber. A malfunctioning EGR valve can affect emissions and engine performance.

Evaporative Emissions (EVAP) System Problem controls the release of fuel vapor from the fuel tank. Problems with the EVAP system can lead to fuel vapor leaks and emissions issues.
