Everything you need to know about alterantor failures:

It's recommended to repair or replace the alternator promptly when these signs appear to prevent further damage to the vehicle's electrical system and ensure reliable performance. Delaying alternator repairs can lead to more extensive and expensive repairs down the line, as well as the risk of being stranded due to a dead battery.

Our Recomendation:

Dimming or Flickering Lights If your headlights or interior lights dim or flicker while the engine is running, it could indicate a problem with the alternator.

Difficulty Starting If your vehicle struggles to start or requires frequent jump starts, the alternator may not be properly charging the battery.

Battery Problems If your battery repeatedly dies or fails to hold a charge, it could be due to a faulty alternator not properly charging the battery while the engine is running.

Electrical Issues: Malfunctions in electrical components such as power windows, radio, or air conditioning could be caused by insufficient power supply from the alternator.
