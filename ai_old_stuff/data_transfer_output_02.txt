A fuel tank is a container storing liquid or compressed fuel for vehicles.
Made of metal or plastic, it's often located in the vehicle's rear or underside.
They come in various shapes and sizes, tailored to different vehicle types.
Designed to withstand pressure and temperature changes for fuel safety.
Equipped with fuel gauges to indicate the amount of fuel remaining.
Modern tanks integrate with fuel pumps and filters for efficiency.
Minimize fuel evaporation and leakage for environmental protection.
Filled through a fuel inlet on the vehicle's exterior.
Inside, baffles prevent fuel sloshing during vehicle movement.
Essential for storing and supplying fuel to the engine.
Sealed to prevent external contaminants from entering.
May have features like anti-siphon devices to deter theft.
Some vehicles have multiple tanks for increased capacity.
Materials are resistant to corrosion and rust for durability.
Shapes are optimized to fit within the vehicle's chassis.
Liquid fuel tanks are vented to allow air to replace consumed fuel.
Compressed gas tanks are designed to withstand higher pressures.
Safety features like pressure relief valves prevent rupture.
Race car tanks are custom-made for weight distribution optimization.
Underground tanks for gas stations resist soil corrosion.
Subject to regulations ensuring safety and environmental impact.
Regular inspections and maintenance prevent failures.
Equipped with quick-release valves for emergencies.
Aircraft tanks are located in wings or fuselage for weight distribution.
Marine vessels' tanks resist seawater corrosion.
May have multiple compartments to prevent spills.
Spacecraft tanks are pressurized for fuel flow in microgravity.
Equipped with sensors to monitor fuel levels and pressure.
Design influences vehicle performance and efficiency.
Factors like weight, volume, and placement are considered.
Electric vehicle tanks store energy in batteries.
Located in the undercarriage or dedicated compartments.
Rechargeable batteries provide propulsion energy.
Unlike traditional tanks, electric vehicle batteries have fixed capacities.
Capacity measured in kilowatt-hours (kWh).
Charging stations replenish energy stored in tanks.
Hybrid vehicle tanks store both fuel and electricity.
Separate compartments for gasoline and batteries.
Optimize fuel usage by switching between power sources.
Integration with vehicle systems is crucial for efficient operation.
Advances improve tank performance.
Research explores alternative fuels and storage methods.
Crucial for transportation industry and daily life.
Enable long-distance travel without frequent refueling.
Vital components ensuring vehicle reliability.
